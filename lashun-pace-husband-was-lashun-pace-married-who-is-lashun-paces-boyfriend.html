    LaShun Pace was a Stellar Award-winning American gospel singer, songwriter and evangelist.

    Tarrian LaShun Pace, otherwise known as LaShun Pace-Rhodes or Shun Pace-Rhodes was born in September 1961, in Atlanta, Georgia, United States to Bishop M.J. Pace and Bettie Ann Pace.

    LaShun Pace was raised in a small community called Poole Creek. She started gaining attention when she performed alongside her siblings in the group The Anointed Pace Sisters (Duranice, Phyllis, June, Melonda, Dejuaii, Leslie, Latrice, and Lydia).

    LaShun Pace was signed as a solo artist in Savoy Records and in 1990, she released her debut He Lives, which reached the number two spot on the Billboard gospel charts. She also wrote For My Good But For His Glory in 2003.

    LaShun Pace was inducted into the Christian Music Hall of Fame in 2007. She was also nominated for Urban Performer of the Year in the Visionary Awards in 2009. On March 21 2022, LaShun Pace passed away, however, the cause of death is still unknown.
