    Who is the American singer SZA?

    SZA is a singer and songwriter based in America. Her music falls in the alternative R&B genre with hip hop, soul, indie rock, minimalist R&B, chillwave, witch house and cloud rap. She started her career around 2010 and achieved a lot over this time.

    Critics describe SZA’s music as unravelling. Her songs touch on the themes of nostalgia, sexuality, and abandonment. SZA’s love for poetry and freestyle influenced her songwriting.

    Biography Timeline

    SZA is an American singer, songwriter, and musician known for her soulful and unique sound. Born Solána Imani Rowe on November 8, 1989, in St. Louis, Missouri, SZA grew up with a love for music and eventually pursued a career in the industry. Here is a detailed biography timeline of SZA.

    • 1989: Solána Imani Rowe was born on November 8 in St. Louis, Missouri.
    • 2011: SZA begins making music and releases her first EP, “See.SZA.Run.”
    • 2012: SZA signs with Top Dawg Entertainment, a record label based in California.
    • 2013: SZA releases her second EP, “S.”
    • 2014: SZA collaborates with Kendrick Lamar on the song “Babylon,” which appears on her third EP, “Z.”
    • 2017: SZA releases her debut studio album, “Ctrl,” which receives critical acclaim and is certified platinum.
    • 2018: SZA receives four Grammy nominations, including Best New Artist, Best Urban Contemporary Album, Best R&B Performance, and Best R&B Song.
    • 2019: SZA collaborates with Travis Scott and The Weeknd on the song “Power Is Power,” which appears on the Game of Thrones soundtrack.
    • 2020: SZA releases the single “Hit Different,” featuring Ty Dolla Sign, which becomes a commercial success.
    • 2021: SZA releases the single “Good Days,” which reaches the top ten on the Billboard Hot 100 chart.

    Career

    SZA started creating music in 2010 and released See.SZA.RUN and S. She released Z after signing with Top Daw Entertainment, a hip-hop record label. Z is her third EP, and it became her debut retail release.

    The first studio album, titled Ctrl, came out in 2017. She got critical acclaim for this album. It was No. 3 on the US Billboard 200 and received the platinum certification from the Recording Industry Association of America.

    SZA had four Grammy Awards nominations along with Best New Artist during the sixteenth yearly program—time listed Ctrl as the best album in 2017.

    SZA appeared on Maroon 5’s What Lovers Do, a Top 10 hit single. She worked with Kendrick Lamar for the Black Panther soundtrack, All The Stars. That song had nominations for the Academy Award for Best Original Song and the Golden Globe Award.

    In 2021, Good Days, SZA’s new single, became famous on different music streaming platforms. It is on the Billboard Hot 100 as the debut solo top hit. At Billboard Women in Music, she got the Rulebreaker Award. In 2018, SZA became the Best New Artist at the BET Awards.
